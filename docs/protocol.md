# Nami Protocol v1.0

## Core Protocol Objects

Nami is built around six foundational objects.

### Identity

Represents a persistent player, developer, creator, moderator, or system participant.

Identity is the root object of the Nami ecosystem.

---

### Passport

Represents progression and achievement.

Passport contains:

* Level
* Experience
* Reputation
* Badges
* Puzzle Pieces
* Prestige Rewards
* Cosmetic Unlocks

---

### Hub

Represents an organizational container.

Examples:

* Studio Hub
* Game Hub
* Creator Hub
* Guild Hub
* Community Hub

Hubs organize Channels and community structure.

---

### Channel

Represents a communication surface within a Hub.

Examples:

* Announcements
* Community Chat
* Support
* Events
* Competitive
* Private
* Gated

Channels always belong to a Hub.

---

### Squad

Represents a trust-based endorsement relationship.

Trusted and Elite members may allocate limited Squad Slots to non-verified members.

Squads provide onboarding, trust signaling, and expanded participation opportunities.

Squad memberships automatically renew while slots remain available.

---

### Guild

Represents a persistent player-created community.

Guild creation requires:

* One Trusted Member or higher
* Two Verified Members
* Guild Creation Eligibility

Guilds act as long-term social organizations within Nami.

---

## Discovery System

Channel discovery is driven by:

* Engagement
* Activity
* Verification Signals
* Community Boosts

Discovery is community-driven and not advertisement-driven.

---

## Boost System

Boosts are identity-bound, non-transferable discovery signals.

Weekly allocation:

Adventurer:

* 1 Boost

Pro:

* 6 Boosts

Elite:

* 8 Boosts

Rules:

* Weekly reset
* No rollover
* Maximum 3 boosts per channel per cycle
* Boosts influence discovery only

Boosts never grant governance, moderation, ownership, or administrative power.

---

## Core Principle

Identity is the foundation of every protocol system.

All protocol objects ultimately attach to Identity.
